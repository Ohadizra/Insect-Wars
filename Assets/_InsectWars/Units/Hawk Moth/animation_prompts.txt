========================================
  HAWK MOTH — ANIMATION PROMPTS
  For Unity AI Animation Generation
========================================

MODEL INFO
----------
Model:          Meshy AI "Nocturnal Sentinel" (FBX)
Rig:            Generic
Scale:          80 (import), prefab visual scale 2x
Orientation:    FBX exports Z-up; runtime applies -90X correction
Unit Role:      Support caster, healer — no attack animations needed
Movement:       Has both ground and air modes (MothStealth state machine)

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR ALL ANIMATIONS
-----------------------------------
- The model faces +Z in its local space (after -90X correction at runtime).
- All animations must loop seamlessly EXCEPT Death.
- Root motion is NOT used — the NavMeshAgent drives translation.
- Keep all animation on the skeleton; do NOT bake root translation.
- Wing bones are the primary expressive element for this unit.
- The moth is delicate and graceful — all motion should feel light and airy,
  never heavy or mechanical.
- Legs should have 6 legs visible (3 pairs), typical of a moth/insect.

========================================
GROUND ANIMATIONS (wings folded, on ground)
========================================

--- IDLE_GROUND ---
Clip Name:      Idle_Ground
Duration:       3-4 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Description:
The moth rests on the ground with wings folded flat against its body, covering
the abdomen like a tent. The pose should resemble a real moth at rest.

Key Motions:
- Wings: Folded back, overlapping slightly, resting against the body.
  Occasional very subtle wing twitch (once per loop, ~0.1s flutter then
  settle back). This is NOT a full wing beat — just a micro-adjustment.
- Antennae: Gentle swaying, feathery antennae drift left-right with slight
  phase offset between the two. Speed ~0.8 Hz.
- Body: Very subtle breathing — thorax expands/contracts at ~0.4 Hz.
  Amplitude is tiny (1-2% scale pulse).
- Legs: Planted firmly. Occasional single-leg micro-adjustment (shift weight)
  once per loop cycle.
- Abdomen: Slight rhythmic pulse synced with breathing.
- Head: Occasional slow turn (look left, pause, look right) over 2 seconds.

Feel: Calm, alert, resting. Like a moth sitting on a wall at night.


--- WALK_GROUND ---
Clip Name:      Walk_Ground
Duration:       1.0-1.2 seconds (one full gait cycle, looping)
Loop:           Yes

Description:
The moth walks on the ground using its six legs in a typical insect tripod
gait. Wings remain folded. Movement is cautious and slightly awkward — moths
are not natural walkers.

Key Motions:
- Legs: Alternating tripod gait (L1+R2+L3 then R1+L2+R3). Each leg lifts,
  reaches forward, plants. Step height ~15% of body height. The gait should
  feel tentative, not confident like an ant.
- Wings: Folded against body, slightly bouncing with each step. NOT beating.
  Very slight wing separation on each footfall impact.
- Body: Forward lean ~10 degrees. Gentle vertical bob synced with leg plants
  (~2 bobs per cycle). Slight lateral sway as weight shifts between tripod
  groups.
- Antennae: Pointing forward and slightly down, probing ahead. More active
  than idle — faster oscillation.
- Abdomen: Drags slightly behind the thorax, swaying with each step.

Feel: Awkward but functional. A flying creature forced to walk.


========================================
FLYING ANIMATIONS (wings active, airborne)
========================================

--- IDLE_FLY ---
Clip Name:      Idle_Fly
Duration:       1.5-2.0 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Description:
The moth hovers in place. Wings beat at a moderate frequency, sustaining
altitude. The body hangs slightly below the wing plane, legs tucked.
This is the moth's default state — it should feel natural and effortless.

Key Motions:
- Wings: Continuous beating at ~6-8 Hz (full up-down stroke cycle).
  Downstroke is faster and more powerful than upstroke. Wing tips trace
  a figure-8 pattern when viewed from the side. Full spread on downstroke,
  slight fold on upstroke. Amplitude: ~60 degrees total arc.
- Body: Gentle vertical bob synced with wingbeats but dampened — the body
  bobs ~5% of wing amplitude. Slight forward tilt (~5 degrees).
- Legs: Tucked close to body, dangling slightly. Occasional micro-twitch.
  NOT extended like landing gear.
- Antennae: Swept back slightly by airflow. Gentle flutter.
- Abdomen: Hangs down slightly, subtle sway.
- Head: Stable, looking forward. Occasional slow scan left/right.

Feel: Effortless hovering. Graceful and ethereal. A creature in its element.


--- WALK_FLY (Move_Fly) ---
Clip Name:      Walk_Fly
Duration:       0.8-1.0 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Description:
The moth flies forward with purpose. Wings beat harder and faster than hover.
The body tilts forward into the direction of travel. This animation plays
when the moth is moving while airborne.

Key Motions:
- Wings: Faster beat than Idle_Fly (~10-12 Hz). Stronger downstroke with
  more pronounced forward sweep on the recovery. Wing amplitude increases
  to ~75 degrees. The wingbeat has a more aggressive, propulsive quality.
- Body: Tilted forward ~15-20 degrees (leaning into flight). Less vertical
  bob than hover — the body is more streamlined.
- Legs: Fully tucked and pressed against body. No dangling.
- Antennae: Swept back along the body by airflow. Minimal movement.
- Abdomen: Extended back, acting as a counterbalance. Slight streamlined
  posture.
- Head: Pointed forward, stable. No scanning — focused on destination.

Feel: Purposeful forward flight. Fast and determined. The body language says
"I'm going somewhere."


========================================
TRANSITION ANIMATIONS
========================================

--- LANDING ---
Clip Name:      Landing
Duration:       0.5 seconds (one-shot, NOT looping)
Loop:           No

Description:
The moth transitions from flight to ground. Wings fold, legs extend, body
rotates from flight tilt to upright ground stance.

Key Motions (in sequence):
- 0.0-0.15s: Wings begin slowing beat frequency. Legs start extending
  downward from tucked position. Body begins rotating from flight tilt
  to upright.
- 0.15-0.35s: Wings transition from beating to spreading wide (braking).
  Legs fully extended below, reaching for ground. Body nearly upright.
  Antennae shift from swept-back to forward-facing.
- 0.35-0.50s: Wings fold down against body into resting position. Legs
  plant on ground (slight bend on contact). Body settles into ground
  idle posture. Gentle bounce on landing impact.

Feel: Gentle descent. Like a leaf settling, not a bird landing.


--- TAKEOFF ---
Clip Name:      Takeoff
Duration:       0.5 seconds (one-shot, NOT looping)
Loop:           No

Description:
Reverse of landing. The moth launches from ground into flight.

Key Motions (in sequence):
- 0.0-0.15s: Wings snap open from folded position. Legs tense and push
  off ground. Body crouches slightly (pre-jump compression). Antennae
  flick forward.
- 0.15-0.30s: Powerful first wingbeat (extra large amplitude). Legs lift
  off ground and begin tucking. Body pitches forward into flight angle.
  Visible upward thrust.
- 0.30-0.50s: Wing beat stabilizes to normal hover frequency. Legs fully
  tucked. Body settles into hover tilt. Transition complete — blends
  into Idle_Fly.

Feel: Explosive but graceful. A burst of energy that quickly settles into
the moth's natural flight rhythm.


========================================
OTHER ANIMATIONS
========================================

--- DEATH ---
Clip Name:      Death
Duration:       0.5 seconds (one-shot, NOT looping)
Loop:           No

Description:
The moth dies and falls. Wings crumple, body goes limp, curls up on impact.
Should work from both flying and grounded states (the MothStealth system
handles the height transition; this animation handles the body collapse).

Key Motions (in sequence):
- 0.0-0.10s: Wings jolt and freeze mid-position (sudden loss of control).
  Body stiffens momentarily. Antennae splay outward.
- 0.10-0.30s: Wings crumple and fold irregularly (NOT symmetrically —
  one wing folds faster than the other). Body begins to curl, legs
  contract inward. Head drops.
- 0.30-0.50s: Full collapse. Body curls into fetal-like position on its
  side. Wings crumpled against body. Legs curled under. Classic dead
  insect pose — on its back or side with legs pulled in.

Feel: Sudden and tragic. A graceful creature losing all its elegance in
an instant.


========================================
ANIMATOR CONTROLLER SETUP
========================================

Controller: Assets/_InsectWars/Animations/HawkMoth.controller

Parameters:
- Speed (Float)       — planar velocity magnitude
- IsMoving (Bool)     — true when unit has movement orders
- IsFlying (Bool)     — true when MothStealth state is Flying
- Death (Trigger)     — triggers death animation

State Machine:
  Default → Idle_Ground

  Idle_Ground → Walk_Ground:  IsMoving == true, IsFlying == false
  Walk_Ground → Idle_Ground:  IsMoving == false, IsFlying == false

  Idle_Ground → Takeoff:      IsFlying == true
  Takeoff → Idle_Fly:         (has exit time = true, exitTime = 0.9)

  Idle_Fly → Walk_Fly:        IsMoving == true, IsFlying == true
  Walk_Fly → Idle_Fly:        IsMoving == false, IsFlying == true

  Idle_Fly → Landing:         IsFlying == false
  Landing → Idle_Ground:      (has exit time = true, exitTime = 0.9)

  Any State → Death:          Death trigger (has exit time = false)

Transition durations: 0.1-0.15s for locomotion blends.
Takeoff/Landing are one-shot states with exit time.
