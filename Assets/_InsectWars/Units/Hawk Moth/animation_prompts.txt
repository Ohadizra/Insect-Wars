========================================
  HAWK MOTH — ANIMATION PROMPTS
  For Unity AI Animation Generation
========================================

MODEL INFO
----------
Model:          Meshy AI "Nocturnal Sentinel" (FBX)
Rig:            Generic
Scale:          80 (import), prefab visual scale 2x
Orientation:    FBX exports Z-up; runtime applies -90X correction
Unit Role:      Support caster, healer — no attack animations needed
Movement:       Has both ground and air modes (MothStealth state machine)

CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS
---------------------
1. WING BONES MUST BE NAMED: "L_wing" / "R_wing" or "wing_L" / "wing_R"
   (so the runtime procedural fallback can find them).
2. The moth MUST look like it is FLYING, not jumping or bouncing.
   Wing motion is the primary visual — wings must flap smoothly.
3. Flying height is handled by code (MothStealth component). Animations
   should NOT include vertical root translation.
4. Team color tinting is applied via MaterialPropertyBlock at runtime.
   The model's base material should remain neutral/white for proper tinting.

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR ALL ANIMATIONS
-----------------------------------
- The model faces +Z in its local space (after -90X correction at runtime).
- All animations must loop seamlessly EXCEPT Death, Landing, Takeoff.
- Root motion is NOT used — the NavMeshAgent drives translation.
- Keep all animation on the skeleton; do NOT bake root translation.
- Wing bones are the primary expressive element for this unit.
- The moth is delicate and graceful — all motion should feel light and airy,
  never heavy or mechanical.
- The moth has large, broad wings (like a hawk moth / sphinx moth).
  Wing beats are NOT fast hummingbird flutters — they are broad, sweeping
  strokes that you can actually SEE.

========================================
FLYING ANIMATIONS (wings active, airborne)
  ** PRIORITY: Generate these FIRST **
========================================

--- IDLE_FLY ---
Clip Name:      Idle_Fly
Duration:       1.5-2.0 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Prompt:
"A large hawk moth hovering in place. Broad wings beat in smooth, sweeping
 up-and-down strokes at roughly 4-6 beats per second. Downstroke is faster
 and more powerful than upstroke. Wings spread wide on downstroke, fold
 slightly on upstroke. Body hangs still and stable beneath the wings with
 only a very subtle vertical bob. Legs tucked close to body. Feathery
 antennae swept back gently. Abdomen hangs down, slight sway.
 The hovering looks effortless and graceful — a creature perfectly at home
 in the air. Smooth and continuous, never jerky or bouncy."

Key Bones:
- L_wing / R_wing: ~60 degree arc, symmetric, figure-8 tip path
- Body/thorax: near-still, <5% vertical bob relative to wing motion
- Legs: tucked, occasional micro-twitch
- Antennae: gentle flutter swept back
- Abdomen: slight pendulum sway

Feel: Effortless hovering. Ethereal and graceful. NOT bouncing/jumping.


--- WALK_FLY (Move_Fly) ---
Clip Name:      Walk_Fly
Duration:       0.8-1.0 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Prompt:
"A large hawk moth flying forward with purpose. Wings beat harder and faster
 than hover — stronger downstroke with forward sweep. Body tilts forward
 15-20 degrees leaning into flight. Less vertical bob than hover — body is
 streamlined. Legs fully tucked against body. Antennae swept back flat.
 Abdomen extended back as counterbalance. The flight looks fast, determined,
 and purposeful. Smooth continuous wing motion."

Key Bones:
- L_wing / R_wing: ~75 degree arc, faster beat, propulsive forward sweep
- Body: 15-20 degree forward tilt
- Legs: pressed tight to body
- Antennae: flat against body
- Abdomen: extended back, streamlined

Feel: Purposeful forward flight. Fast and determined.


========================================
GROUND ANIMATIONS (wings folded, on ground)
========================================

--- IDLE_GROUND ---
Clip Name:      Idle_Ground
Duration:       3-4 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

Prompt:
"A large moth resting on the ground with wings folded flat against its body
 like a tent over its abdomen. Very still. Feathery antennae sway gently
 left-right. Subtle breathing — thorax expands and contracts slightly.
 Occasional single wing twitch (very brief flutter then settle, once per loop).
 Legs planted firmly, occasional micro-adjustment. Head turns slowly to look
 around. Calm, alert, resting. Like a moth sitting on a wall at night."

Key Bones:
- L_wing / R_wing: folded flat, one brief twitch per cycle
- Antennae: gentle swaying, phase offset between left/right
- Body/thorax: subtle breathing pulse
- Legs: planted, occasional weight shift
- Head: slow left-right scanning

Feel: Calm, alert, resting.


--- WALK_GROUND ---
Clip Name:      Walk_Ground
Duration:       1.0-1.2 seconds (one full gait cycle, looping)
Loop:           Yes

Prompt:
"A large moth walking on the ground using six legs in alternating tripod gait.
 Wings remain folded against body, slightly bouncing with each step.
 Movement is cautious and slightly awkward — moths are not natural walkers.
 Forward lean about 10 degrees. Gentle vertical bob synced with leg plants.
 Antennae point forward probing ahead. Abdomen drags slightly behind,
 swaying with steps."

Key Bones:
- Legs: alternating tripod gait (L1+R2+L3 then R1+L2+R3)
- L_wing / R_wing: folded, slight bounce on footfalls
- Body: 10 degree forward lean, vertical bob with steps
- Antennae: forward, active probing
- Abdomen: trailing sway

Feel: Awkward but functional. A flying creature forced to walk.


========================================
TRANSITION ANIMATIONS
========================================

--- LANDING ---
Clip Name:      Landing
Duration:       0.5 seconds (one-shot, NOT looping)
Loop:           No

Prompt:
"A moth transitioning from flight to ground. Wings slow their beating and
 spread wide for braking. Legs extend downward from tucked position. Body
 rotates from flight tilt to upright. Wings fold down against body. Legs
 plant with gentle bend on contact. Gentle settling motion like a leaf."

Sequence:
- 0.0-0.15s: Wings slow beat, legs extend, body uprights
- 0.15-0.35s: Wings spread wide (braking), legs reach for ground
- 0.35-0.50s: Wings fold to rest, legs plant, body settles


--- TAKEOFF ---
Clip Name:      Takeoff
Duration:       0.5 seconds (one-shot, NOT looping)
Loop:           No

Prompt:
"A moth launching from the ground into flight. Wings snap open from folded
 position. Legs push off and tuck. First wingbeat is extra large and powerful.
 Body pitches forward into flight angle. Quick burst of energy that settles
 into smooth hovering."

Sequence:
- 0.0-0.15s: Wings snap open, legs tense, body crouches
- 0.15-0.30s: Powerful first wingbeat, legs lift and tuck, body pitches forward
- 0.30-0.50s: Wing beat stabilizes, legs tucked, blends to Idle_Fly

========================================
ANIMATOR CONTROLLER SETUP
========================================

Controller: Assets/_InsectWars/Animations/HawkMoth.controller

Parameters:
- Speed (Float)       — planar velocity magnitude
- IsMoving (Bool)     — true when unit has movement orders
- IsFlying (Bool)     — true when MothStealth state is Flying/Landing
- Death (Trigger)     — triggers death animation

State Machine:
  Default → Idle_Fly  (moth spawns airborne)

  Idle_Fly → Walk_Fly:        IsMoving == true
  Walk_Fly → Idle_Fly:        IsMoving == false

  Idle_Fly → Landing:         IsFlying == false
  Landing → Idle_Ground:      (has exit time = true, exitTime = 0.9)

  Idle_Ground → Walk_Ground:  IsMoving == true, IsFlying == false
  Walk_Ground → Idle_Ground:  IsMoving == false

  Idle_Ground → Takeoff:      IsFlying == true
  Takeoff → Idle_Fly:         (has exit time = true, exitTime = 0.9)

  Any State → Death:          Death trigger (has exit time = false)

Transition durations: 0.1-0.15s for locomotion blends.
Takeoff/Landing are one-shot states with exit time.

========================================
BONE NAMING CONVENTION (REQUIRED)
========================================

The runtime code searches for these exact bone names
(UnitAnimationDriver.FindRecursive):

  Wings:  "L_wing"  / "R_wing"
          "wing_L"  / "wing_R"
          "LeftWing" / "RightWing"
          "Wing_L"  / "Wing_R"

  Head:   "head"
  Chest:  "chest"
  Tail:   "tail"
  Legs:   "frontleg" / "R_frontleg"

If the model uses different names, rename the bones in the FBX
or add aliases to UnitAnimationDriver.Awake().
