  For Unity AI Animation Generation

MODEL INFO
----------
Model:          Meshy AI "Rusty Stag in the Jungle" (FBX, quadruped rig)
Rig:            Generic
Scale:          80 (import), prefab visual scale 3x
Orientation:    FBX exports Z-up; runtime applies -90X correction
Unit Role:      Heavy tank / frontline bruiser — slow, powerful melee with AoE ground stomp
Movement:       Ground only, slowest unit in the game (speed 2.8)
Walking Anim:   Already provided (Walking_withSkin.fbx) — looping walk cycle

CRITICAL REQUIREMENTS
---------------------
1. The stag beetle is MASSIVE and HEAVY. Every motion must convey weight
   and power. No quick, nimble movements — everything is deliberate.
2. The large mandibles (antler-like pincers) are the primary attack weapon
   and should be the focal point of the Attack animation.
3. The Ground Stomp is the signature ability — it must feel like an
   earthquake-causing impact that shakes the ground.
4. Six legs on the model — leg animations should feel heavy and planted,
   gripping the ground firmly.
5. The armored carapace barely flexes — most expression comes from legs,
   mandibles, and head movement.
6. Team color tinting is applied via MaterialPropertyBlock at runtime.
   The model's base material should remain neutral for proper tinting.

IMPORTANT NOTES FOR ALL ANIMATIONS
-----------------------------------
- The model faces +Z in its local space (after -90X correction at runtime).
- All animations must loop seamlessly EXCEPT Death.
- Root motion is NOT used — the NavMeshAgent drives translation.
- Keep all animation on the skeleton; do NOT bake root translation.
- The beetle has thick, spiky legs and a wide, low stance. It is the
  heaviest creature on the battlefield.
- Mandibles are the large curved pincers extending from the head.
- The carapace (back shell) should have minimal deformation.

========================================
ATTACK ANIMATION
  ** PRIORITY: Generate this FIRST **
========================================

--- ATTACK ---
Clip Name:      Attack
Duration:       0.35-0.45 seconds (looping)
Loop:           Yes

NOTE: This attack follows the SAME pattern as the Mantis Fighter and other
melee units — a fast forward lunge with squash-and-stretch. The beetle's
mandibles open during the lunge to add its own flavor, but the core motion
is identical to the standard melee attack so all units feel consistent.

Prompt:
"A giant armored stag beetle performing a fast forward lunge attack.
 The entire body LUNGES forward along its facing direction — a sharp,
 explosive thrust. At the same time the body squashes vertically and
 stretches horizontally (squash-and-stretch cartoon principle) to sell
 the impact. The mandibles snap open wide during the lunge peak, then
 clamp shut as the body returns. The front legs extend forward with the
 lunge. The motion is quick and punchy — roughly 0.4 seconds total.
 Think of the same attack motion as a mantis fighter, but heavier and
 with mandibles opening. The beetle springs forward, crunches the target,
 and snaps back to ready stance."

Key Bones:
- Body/root: Translate forward ~0.45 units along facing, sinusoidal arc
- Body scale: Squash Y (compress height), stretch X+Z (widen) at lunge peak
- Front legs (arms): Swing forward ~85 degrees during lunge
- Mandibles (L/R): Open 40 degrees at lunge peak, close on return
- Head: Follows body momentum, slight forward dip
- Middle/rear legs: Stay planted, absorb momentum

Timing:
- 0.00-0.15s: Lunge forward, body squash begins, mandibles open
- 0.15-0.25s: Peak lunge, maximum squash-stretch, mandibles widest
- 0.25-0.40s: Snap back to neutral, mandibles close, body unsquashes
- Easing: Sinusoidal (smooth in, smooth out) — same curve as Mantis

========================================
GROUND STOMP ANIMATION
  ** PRIORITY: Generate this SECOND **
========================================

--- GROUND_STOMP ---
Clip Name:      GroundStomp
Duration:       1.5-2.0 seconds (single play, not looping)
Loop:           No

Prompt:
"A massive stag beetle performing a powerful ground stomp that shakes the
 earth. The beetle first rears up, lifting its front two legs off the ground
 while the rear four legs brace (0.5s wind-up). The front body rises about
 20-25 degrees. Mandibles spread wide threateningly. Then the beetle SLAMS
 its front body and legs down into the ground with enormous force (0.3s
 impact). On impact, the whole body compresses down — the carapace dips,
 all six legs bend and absorb the shockwave. The ground impact should feel
 like a small earthquake. After impact, the body slowly rises back to
 standing height with a heavy, labored recovery (0.7s recovery).

 The stomp is the beetle's signature move — it should look like it could
 crack stone. The anticipation (rearing up) is dramatic, the impact is
 sudden and violent, and the recovery is slow and heavy. Think of a
 sumo wrestler stomping — raw mass and power."

Key Bones:
- Front legs (pair 1): Lift completely off ground during wind-up,
  then SLAM down hard. Claws splay on impact.
- Middle legs (pair 2): Brace wider during wind-up, compress on impact
- Rear legs (pair 3): Act as pivot point, barely lift. Brace outward.
- Head: Tilts UP during wind-up, then drives DOWN with the slam
- Mandibles: Spread wide during wind-up, snap partially on impact
  (like a war cry into a focused blow)
- Body/thorax: Rises 20-25 degrees, then crashes down past neutral
  by 5-10 degrees (compression), then returns to neutral
- Carapace: Subtle compression ripple on impact

Timing:
- 0.00-0.50s: Wind-up — front rises, legs lift, mandibles spread
- 0.50-0.80s: SLAM — everything crashes down, maximum impact
- 0.80-0.90s: Compression — body dips below neutral, absorbing shock
- 0.90-1.70s: Recovery — slow rise back to neutral standing pose
- Easing: Slow dramatic wind-up, INSTANT acceleration into slam,
  sharp stop on impact, slow ease-out recovery

DEATH ANIMATION (optional)
========================================

--- DEATH ---
Clip Name:      Death
Duration:       2.0-2.5 seconds (one-shot, no loop)
Loop:           No

Prompt:
"A giant stag beetle collapsing from fatal damage. The legs on one side
 buckle first (right side), causing the heavy body to list to that side.
 The mandibles go slack, falling open. The remaining legs try to
 compensate but give out one by one. The beetle rolls onto its side
 with a heavy, final crash — the armored carapace hitting the ground
 with visible weight. Legs curl inward (death curl, like real beetles).
 The mandibles give one last weak open-close, then go still. The whole
 death is HEAVY and SLOW — like a tank being disabled, not a creature
 being swatted. It takes time for this much mass to fall."

Key Bones:
- Right legs (3): Buckle sequentially, 0.3s apart
- Left legs (3): Compensate, then fold 0.5s after right side
- Body: List right 30 degrees, then roll to 80-90 degrees (on side)
- Head: Drops, follows body roll
- Mandibles: Fall slack open, one weak closure at 1.5s
- Carapace: Follows body rotation, hits ground

Timing:
- 0.00-0.40s: Right legs buckle, body starts listing
- 0.40-0.80s: Left legs fail, body rolling
- 0.80-1.40s: Full collapse onto side, ground impact
- 1.40-2.00s: Last mandible twitch, legs curl inward, stillness
