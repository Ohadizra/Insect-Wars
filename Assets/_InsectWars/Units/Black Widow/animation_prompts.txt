========================================
  BLACK WIDOW — UNITY AI ANIMATION PROMPTS
========================================

CRITICAL MODEL NOTES — READ BEFORE ANIMATING:
  The source FBX model (Crimson Widow) has its rest pose facing
  downward along -Y. In the game prefab, a -90 X rotation on
  the Visual root corrects this so the spider stands upright at
  runtime. ALL animations must be authored in the MODEL'S local
  space (i.e. the spider's belly faces +Y in the FBX). The
  prefab rotation handles the rest.

  The model has 8 legs, a compact front section (cephalothorax),
  and a large rear section (abdomen). Legs are numbered L1-L4
  and R1-R4 from front to back.

  If the generated animation produces a face-down or collapsed
  pose, the bind pose is wrong — the spider must stand upright
  on all eight legs in its rest/idle pose FIRST before any
  motion is applied.

Target: Unity 6 Muse Animate or equivalent AI animation tool.
        Each animation should be exported as a separate .anim clip.

Animator Parameters:
  - Speed     (float)   — planar velocity magnitude
  - IsMoving  (bool)    — true when speed > 0.1
  - Attack    (trigger) — melee bite
  - WebCast   (trigger) — web net ability
  - Death     (trigger) — unit killed


─────────────────────────────────────
1. IDLE  (looping)
─────────────────────────────────────
Prompt:
  "Create a looping idle animation for a black widow spider model.
   The spider must be standing upright on all eight legs in a wide,
   stable stance — NOT collapsed, NOT face-down, NOT in a T-pose.
   The legs should be bent at the joints with feet planted on the
   ground plane, body raised above the ground.

   Subtle idle motion: the entire body gently rises and falls by
   about 2-3% of body height, like slow breathing. The front pair
   of legs (L1, R1) slightly lift and tap back down in an
   alternating rhythm, as if sensing the environment. The abdomen
   has a very gentle side-to-side sway. All motion is subtle and
   slow — the spider is alert and waiting, not twitching.

   The default standing height should place the body center roughly
   at 1/3 of the total leg span above the ground. Legs should be
   angled outward like a real spider's resting stance."

Clip name: BlackWidow_Idle
Loop: Yes
Duration: ~2.5 seconds
IMPORTANT: This is the default state. If this pose is wrong (face-down,
           collapsed), every other animation will be broken. Get the
           standing pose right first.


─────────────────────────────────────
2. WALK  (looping)
─────────────────────────────────────
Prompt:
  "Create a looping walk cycle for a black widow spider model.
   The spider walks forward using a real spider's alternating
   tetrapod gait: legs move in two groups of four.

   Group A (moves together): L1, R2, L3, R4
   Group B (moves together): R1, L2, R3, L4
   While Group A steps forward, Group B stays planted, and vice
   versa. Each leg lifts, swings forward in an arc, then plants
   down. The swing height is about half the body height.

   The body glides forward smoothly with very little vertical
   bounce (no more than 5% of body height). The abdomen trails
   behind with a very slight lateral wobble. The cephalothorax
   stays level and pointed forward.

   The body must remain upright and elevated on the legs throughout
   the entire cycle — the spider should NEVER collapse or sag to
   the ground during the walk. One full left-right step cycle in
   approximately 0.8 seconds at normal speed."

Clip name: BlackWidow_Walk
Loop: Yes
Duration: ~0.8 seconds (one full step cycle)
Speed parameter: Playback rate scaled by the Speed animator float.
IMPORTANT: The spider must stay upright. If legs clip through the ground
           or the body drops, adjust leg joint angles.


─────────────────────────────────────
3. ATTACK — Melee Bite  (one-shot)
─────────────────────────────────────
Prompt:
  "Create a melee bite attack animation for a black widow spider.
   Starting from the upright idle stance:

   Frames 0-4 (windup, ~0.1s): The front two pairs of legs (L1, R1,
   L2, R2) lift slightly and spread apart. The cephalothorax tips
   downward toward the target.

   Frames 4-8 (strike, ~0.15s): The entire front body LUNGES forward
   sharply. The fangs open wide and snap shut. The front legs slam
   down around the target point in a grabbing motion.

   Frames 8-12 (retract, ~0.15s): The spider pulls back to its
   upright stance. The fangs close. All legs return to their idle
   positions.

   The strike should feel FAST and violent — a predator biting prey.
   The abdomen barely moves during the attack, only the front half
   of the body drives forward. Keep the rear legs firmly planted
   as anchors throughout."

Clip name: BlackWidow_Attack
Loop: No
Duration: ~0.40 seconds
Trigger: "Attack" animator trigger
Notes: Plays each time the unit deals melee damage (every 1.1s cooldown).
       The unit is position-locked and facing the target during this clip.


─────────────────────────────────────
4. WEB CAST — Web Net Ability  (one-shot)
─────────────────────────────────────
Prompt:
  "Create a web-casting animation for a black widow spider — the
   spider is shooting a burst of sticky silk webbing from its
   spinnerets toward enemies in front of it.

   Starting from the upright idle stance:

   Frames 0-4 (rear up, ~0.1s): The spider lifts its front body
   upward. Front legs (L1, R1, L2, R2) raise off the ground and
   spread wide. The rear legs push the body up and backward
   slightly, tilting the abdomen forward and upward.

   Frames 4-10 (web release, ~0.2s): The abdomen PULSES forward
   with two rapid contractions — like squeezing out silk. The
   spinnerets at the tip of the abdomen point toward the front/
   target direction. The front legs make a wide sweeping motion
   outward from center, as if spreading and directing strands of
   web into a net shape. The legs should move like they are
   pulling and stretching sticky silk threads apart.

   Frames 10-15 (settle, ~0.2s): The spider drops back to all
   eight legs. Front legs pull back in. Body returns to normal
   upright idle height.

   The key visual feel: the spider is WEAVING — the front legs
   actively manipulate invisible silk strands, spreading them
   outward in a fan/cone. It should look like a spider doing
   what spiders do — working with web. Not just a generic
   magic cast pose."

Clip name: BlackWidow_WebCast
Loop: No
Duration: ~0.50 seconds
Trigger: "WebCast" animator trigger
Notes: Auto-cast every 10 seconds during combat. A particle VFX cone
       of white silk strands is spawned simultaneously by code.


─────────────────────────────────────
5. DEATH  (one-shot)
─────────────────────────────────────
Prompt:
  "Create a death animation for a black widow spider.
   Starting from the upright idle stance:

   Frames 0-5 (collapse, ~0.2s): The legs lose strength. The body
   drops toward the ground as leg joints buckle. The spider tips
   to one side (e.g. slightly to the right).

   Frames 5-15 (death curl, ~0.3s): All eight legs slowly curl
   inward toward the body center — this is the classic real-world
   spider death pose. The legs fold UNDERNEATH the body, joints
   bending so the leg tips point inward and upward. The abdomen
   rolls to rest on the ground. The cephalothorax tilts sideways.

   The final resting pose: the spider is on its side or back with
   all legs curled tightly against the body, motionless. Hold this
   pose on the last keyframe.

   The motion should feel like the life draining out — a gradual
   crumple, not a violent ragdoll. Gravity pulls the body down
   as the legs lose the ability to support it."

Clip name: BlackWidow_Death
Loop: No
Duration: ~0.50 seconds
Trigger: "Death" animator trigger
Notes: After the clip ends, the GameObject is destroyed after a short
       delay. The final curled pose must hold as the last keyframe.

========================================
  ANIMATOR CONTROLLER STRUCTURE
========================================

Controller: Assets/_InsectWars/Animations/BlackWidow.controller

States:
  - Idle      (default state, plays BlackWidow_Idle, looping)
  - Walk      (plays BlackWidow_Walk, looping)
  - Attack    (plays BlackWidow_Attack, returns to Idle/Walk)
  - WebCast   (plays BlackWidow_WebCast, returns to Idle/Walk)
  - Death     (plays BlackWidow_Death, no exit)

Transitions:
  Idle -> Walk:     IsMoving == true
  Walk -> Idle:     IsMoving == false
  Any  -> Attack:   Attack trigger
  Any  -> WebCast:  WebCast trigger
  Any  -> Death:    Death trigger (no exit transition)
  Attack -> Idle:   Has Exit Time (clip end)
  WebCast -> Idle:  Has Exit Time (clip end)


========================================
  TROUBLESHOOTING / FIX CHECKLIST
========================================

If the spider appears face-down or collapsed in-game:
  1. Check that the Idle animation's first keyframe has the spider
     standing upright on all 8 legs, NOT in the FBX bind/rest pose.
  2. The prefab Visual root has localEulerAngles = (-90, 0, 0).
     Animations must NOT fight this rotation — animate in model space.
  3. If the FBX has no embedded clips, the Idle state falls back to
     the Walk clip at speed=0 (frozen first frame). Make sure that
     first frame is a valid standing pose.

If the walk looks like sliding/moonwalking:
  1. The Walk clip should have root motion disabled (handled by
     NavMeshAgent). The legs animate in-place; the agent moves
     the transform.
  2. Check that "IsMoving" bool is being set by UnitAnimationDriver
     — if it's never true, the Idle state plays forever.

If the web cast looks like a generic magic cast:
  1. The front legs must visibly SPREAD outward like pulling silk.
  2. The abdomen must PULSE — two quick forward thrusts.
  3. Think "spider weaving a net with its legs" not "wizard waving hands."
