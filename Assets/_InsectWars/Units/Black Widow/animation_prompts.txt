PROBLEM: The current walk animation does not use all 8 legs. The legs

FIX PROMPT:
  "Fix the walk cycle for this 8-legged spider model. The current
   animation fails to move all 8 legs — this must be corrected.

   EVERY leg must visibly lift, swing forward, and plant down during
   the walk cycle. No leg should remain static or frozen.

   Use a real spider's ALTERNATING TETRAPOD gait pattern:

   PHASE 1 (first half of cycle):
     LIFTING group: L1 (front-left), R2 (mid-right), L3 (mid-left),
     R4 (rear-right) — these four legs lift up, swing forward through
     the air, and plant down ahead of their starting position.
     PLANTED group: R1, L2, R3, L4 — these four stay on the ground
     and push the body forward.

   PHASE 2 (second half of cycle):
     LIFTING group: R1, L2, R3, L4 — now these four lift and swing.
     PLANTED group: L1, R2, L3, R4 — now these push.

   Each individual leg motion has three parts:
     1. LIFT — the leg pulls up off the ground, knee bending upward.
        Lift height is about 30-40% of the body's standing height.
     2. SWING — the leg reaches forward in a smooth arc while airborne.
        The foot tip traces a half-oval path through the air.
     3. PLANT — the leg extends down and forward, foot touches the
        ground ahead of where it started. The leg then pushes backward
        as the body moves over it.

   Body motion during walk:
     - Very slight up-down bob (about 5% of body height) as weight
       shifts between leg groups.
     - The abdomen trails with a subtle left-right sway, lagging
       slightly behind the cephalothorax direction.
     - The cephalothorax stays level and faces forward.
     - The body center maintains consistent height — no sagging or
       collapsing at any point in the cycle.

   The overall feel should be: a large, heavy spider moving with
   deliberate, methodical steps. Each leg placement is precise.
   Think of a tarantula walking — not a frantic small spider, but
   a large predator that places each foot carefully.

   Loop seamlessly. One complete cycle (all 8 legs have stepped once)
   in approximately 0.8-1.0 seconds."
